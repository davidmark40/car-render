Step 7: Return the Car

When your trip comes to an end, return the car to the rental office. Make sure to refuel before you return it, as many rental agencies require this. A quick inspection will be done to ensure the car is in the same condition as when you picked it up.

Tips for Driving in Dubai

Before you zoom off in your rented car, here are a few things you should know to make your driving experience in Dubai more enjoyable:

1. Stay Within the Speed Limit

Dubai has strict traffic laws, and speed cameras are everywhere. If you get caught speeding, you’ll not only face a hefty fine but also risk damaging your driving record. Stick to the speed limits, and you’ll be fine!

2. Parking Fees

While parking spaces are abundant in Dubai, they often come with a fee. Pay attention to the parking meters, especially in busy areas like Downtown Dubai. For parking at shopping malls, you might get a few hours of free parking, but beyond that, it’s time to pay.

3. Avoid Rush Hour

Dubai’s traffic can get heavy during peak hours, usually from 7:30 AM to 9:30 AM and 4:30 PM to 6:30 PM. If possible, try to plan your drives outside of these times to avoid getting stuck in traffic.

4. Use a GPS or Navigation App

Getting lost in Dubai can be frustrating, especially since the city is constantly changing with new roads and developments. Use a GPS device or a navigation app like Google Maps to help you get around. Most rental cars come with GPS systems, but it’s always good to have a backup.

5. Know the Road Signs

Dubai uses a mix of Arabic and English for road signs, so it’s a good idea to familiarize yourself with basic road symbols. Don’t worry—if you’re ever unsure, Google will always be there to help!
